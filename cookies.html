What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored in your browser when you visit a website. They can store session state, security tokens, or preference data. Cookies are set either by the website you are visiting (first-party) or by third-party services embedded in the page.

This policy explains which cookies orthonode.xyz sets, why, and how you can control them.

// 02

Cookies we use

orthonode.xyz is a static website with no server-side session management, no user accounts, and no analytics platform. The only cookie in use is set by Cloudflare Turnstile, a human verification challenge used on the homepage and the Nexus Protocol page to prevent automated abuse.

Name Provider Purpose Duration Type
cf_clearance Cloudflare Stores proof that the visitor has passed the Turnstile human verification challenge. Prevents repeat challenges during the same session. Session Essential
__cf_bm Cloudflare Short-lived bot management token set by Cloudflare during Turnstile verification. Used to distinguish human visitors from automated traffic. 30 minutes Essential
(none) ORTHONODE SYSTEMS™ We do not set any first-party cookies. No analytics, no preferences, no session tokens. — N/A

We do not use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, Hotjar, Mixpanel, or any other analytics or advertising platform. No visitor data is collected, stored, or sold.

// 03

Local storage and session storage

Cloudflare Turnstile may use browser localStorage or sessionStorage internally to manage its verification state. This is limited to the duration of your visit and contains no personal data beyond a verification token.

We do not write to localStorage or sessionStorage ourselves.

// 04

Why there is no cookie consent banner

Under the EU ePrivacy Directive and GDPR, cookies that are strictly necessary for the operation of the service do not require prior consent. Cloudflare Turnstile cookies fall into this category — the site cannot function without bot protection on the protected pages.

Because we set no analytics, advertising, or preference cookies, no consent banner is legally required. We believe this is also the right approach for user experience — unnecessary popups degrade the visit for no benefit.

// 05

How to control cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling the Cloudflare Turnstile cookies will prevent human verification from completing, making the protected pages inaccessible.

01 Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data → See all site data and permissions. Search for orthonode.xyz and delete.
02 Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Manage Data. Search and remove orthonode.xyz.
03 Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data. Search for orthonode.xyz and remove.
04 Cloudflare Privacy: For details on how Cloudflare handles data via Turnstile, see the Cloudflare Privacy Policy ↗ . Turnstile is designed to be privacy-preserving and does not require cookies in all configurations.
