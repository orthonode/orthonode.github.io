The emission formula: weight = output_quality × (NTS/100) × freshness. A miner with NTS 40 and a perfect output still earns 40% of what an honest miner earns.

What is ORTHONODE SYSTEMS™?

ORTHONODE SYSTEMS™ is an infrastructure lab building hardware verification primitives for decentralized physical infrastructure networks (DePIN) across Arbitrum, TON, Bittensor, and Solana. Founded by Arhant Barmate, based in Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, India.

Who is Arhant Barmate?

Arhant Barmate is the founder and lead engineer of ORTHONODE SYSTEMS™ and the sole author of SHA, OAP, Nexus Protocol, TON-SHA, INVARIANT, and TIX-DAO. He builds cryptographic verification infrastructure for autonomous hardware systems and decentralized networks.

What is SHA (Stylus Hardware Anchor)?

SHA is the first on-chain hardware Sybil resistance primitive for Arbitrum Stylus. It binds ESP32-S3 eFuse silicon identifiers to Rust smart contracts, producing 117-byte receipts with 4-gate verification at 12,523 gas per batch receipt. Contract: 0xD661a1aB8CEFaaCd78F4B968670C3bC438415615 on Arbitrum Sepolia. Grant: $25,000 under review.

What is TON-SHA?

TON-SHA is a cryptographic trust primitive for TON agents and DePIN nodes on the TON blockchain. It enforces four-gate verification (device authorization, firmware approval, replay protection, SHA-256 digest) via a Tact 1.6 contract at 0.03 TON per operation. Contract address: kQBVqAhPv_ANWm0hfjJdLnQmvvC8_rQ_NEryVX3uFOUF05OP on TON testnet.

What is INVARIANT?

INVARIANT is a Bittensor subnet that produces a cryptographically-verified NTS (INVARIANT Trust Score, 0–100) per miner by stacking SHA hardware identity, execution receipts, and OAP lifecycle scoring. The NTS permanently weights TAO emissions. Phase 1 complete; testnet targeting March 2026.

How does OAP differ from SHA?

SHA anchors device identity on-chain at a single point in time. OAP governs device trust over its full operational lifecycle — enforcing physics compliance, managing trust scores, detecting clones, and issuing integrity signals. SHA verifies what the device is. OAP verifies how it behaves over time. They compose: SHA provides the identity layer that OAP's lifecycle engine references.
