// THE_PROBLEM

Decentralized systems assume trust.
We verify it.

Every DePIN node, AI agent, and on-chain service makes claims about its hardware, execution environment, and identity. None of them can prove it without primitives that exist at the silicon layer.

01 // IDENTITY

Unverified Hardware Identity

DePIN nodes claim hardware specs with no cryptographic proof. Clone attacks, emulation, and spoofing go undetected at the contract layer.

02 // EXECUTION

Unattested AI Execution

Bittensor validators cannot distinguish genuine inference from fabricated outputs. No receipt mechanism exists at the subnet level for hardware-bound proof.

03 // GOVERNANCE

Centralized Venue Control

$23B live-events market runs on opaque ticketing systems with no on-chain governance, no community treasury, and no verifiable provenance for ticket issuance.

// OPERATING_DOCTRINE

How we build.

Fail-Closed Always

Every system defaults to denial. Verification is a prerequisite, not a checkpoint. Root cause only — no workarounds.

Silicon to Chain

Identity anchored in eFuse hardware, carried through the attestation layer, and verified on-chain. No gaps in the chain of custody.

Multi-Chain by Design

Arbitrum, TON, Polkadot, Bittensor, Solana. Each chain chosen for its verification primitive fit — not portfolio diversification.

No Mainnet Without Proof

Every deployment is preceded by documented testing, stress tests, and explicit founder sign-off. Testnet is not a shortcut.
