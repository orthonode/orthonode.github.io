THE_GAP

SHA Anchors Identity. OAP Governs What Happens Next.

SHA verifies what a device is at a single point in time. But devices operate over months or years — firmware changes, behavior drifts, clones appear, sensors degrade. No existing protocol governs device trust across its full lifecycle.

OAP closes that gap: append-only behavioral history, deterministic physics compliance checks, and lifecycle trust scoring that cannot be reset or manipulated.

THE_PRIMITIVE

Verifiable Integrity State, Not Economic Control

OAP produces one output: a verifiable integrity signal representing the device's current trust state on a 0–100 scale. Partners apply economic logic — reward multipliers, access gates, compliance reports — on top of that signal.

OAP does not control rewards, governance, or economic decisions. It produces the truth. What you do with it is yours.

WHAT_OAP_IS_NOT

No Oracle, No Centralized Authority

OAP is not a centralized reputation system, an oracle service, or a blockchain-specific module. It carries no economic stake, issues no tokens, and maintains no privileged keys. All governance logic is deterministic and auditable. Designed to compose with SHA on Arbitrum, TON-SHA on TON, and any future verification layer.

Primitive 01

Physics Profiles

Deterministic motion validation against hardware-specific physics envelopes. Each device class has a profile defining legal motion bounds — acceleration, angular rate, thermal range. Violations flag immediately and contribute to lifecycle scoring.

Primitive 02

Trust Lifecycle Scoring

Append-only behavioral history produces a 0–100 integrity score across the device's full operational life. Each event — compliance, violation, override — is logged immutably. Past behavior permanently shapes future trust. Cannot be reset.

Primitive 03

Clone Containment

Cross-reference detection flags devices that share identity claims with concurrently active units. Statistical behavioral divergence analysis identifies hardware-level impersonation — cloned silicon cannot maintain the same behavioral fingerprint across independent physics channels.

Primitive 04

Adaptive Anchoring

Trust anchors recalibrate dynamically as the device ages and its behavioral baseline evolves. New calibration requires verified SHA receipt sequences — preventing arbitrary anchor reset. Adaptive anchoring accounts for legitimate sensor drift without opening a reset attack vector.

Primitive 05

Override Governance

A maximum of two operator overrides per device per year are permitted. Each override is logged immutably with timestamp, operator identity, and justification hash. Overrides cannot be cleared, backdated, or issued anonymously. The cap is protocol-enforced.

Composition

OAP + SHA = Complete Trust Stack

SHA provides the identity root — immutable at the silicon layer. OAP governs what that identity does over time. Together they produce a complete hardware trust primitive: point-in-time verification plus lifecycle behavioral governance.

// FREQUENTLY_ASKED_QUESTIONS

What is OAP and how does it differ from SHA?

OAP (Orthonode Assurance Platform) is a chain-agnostic integrity governance engine for DePIN hardware. SHA verifies what a device is at a single moment — immutable silicon identity bound to a smart contract. OAP governs how that device behaves over its full operational lifetime — physics compliance, behavioral history, trust scoring 0–100, clone detection. They compose: SHA provides the identity root that OAP's lifecycle engine references. Together they form a complete hardware trust stack.

Is OAP open-source? Can I integrate it today?

OAP architecture is published and the surface-level integration model is documented. The implementation is PROPRIETARY — internals are not open-sourced. Integration discussions are open for qualified partners. Contact infrastructure@orthonode.xyz with your use case. OAP is chain-agnostic by design — it does not require Arbitrum, TON, or any specific blockchain to operate, though it composes naturally with SHA on Arbitrum and TON-SHA on TON.

What does "OAP emits verifiable integrity state" mean in practice?

OAP's output is a cryptographically verifiable integrity signal on a 0–100 scale representing the device's current trust state based on its full behavioral history. OAP does not control rewards, governance tokens, or economic decisions — those belong to the protocol or application layer above. Partners consume the OAP signal and apply their own economic logic: reward multipliers, access gating, compliance reporting. OAP is a trust primitive, not an economic protocol.

What is the Override Governance cap and why does it exist?

A maximum of two operator overrides per device per year are permitted by protocol. Each override is logged immutably — timestamp, operator identity, justification hash — and cannot be cleared, backdated, or issued anonymously. The cap prevents override abuse as an attack vector (e.g., repeatedly resetting a compromised device's trust score). Override governance is enforced at the protocol level, not via policy — it is not configurable.
