1. Data Collection & Processing

Orthonode Infrastructure Labs operates on a data minimization principle. For hardware verification protocols (SHA - Stylus Hardware Anchor), we process only cryptographic attestations derived from device hardware identifiers (eFuse MAC addresses, chip IDs). No personal identifiable information (PII) is collected, stored, or processed during standard protocol operations.

For research modules (OAP - Orthonode Assurance Platform), telemetry data submitted for verification analysis is processed in volatile memory or isolated compute environments. We require only telemetric primitives: Time, GPS Latitude/Longitude, Speed, IMU/Accelerometer data. We do not require, request, or store driver names, vehicle license plates, financial records, or other PII.

2. Data Lifecycle & Retention

Hardware Verification (SHA): Cryptographic proofs are recorded on-chain (Arbitrum blockchain) as permanent, immutable records. Off-chain metadata (if any) is retained for a maximum of 90 days for audit purposes, then automatically purged.

Telemetry Analysis (OAP): Raw coordinate data is automatically deleted within a maximum retention window of 30 days, unless a shorter period is contractually agreed. We do not retain historical route maps of client assets. Verification results (risk scores, anomaly flags) may be retained for statistical analysis but are anonymized and aggregated.

3. Data Isolation & Security

All verification workloads run in isolated containers with strict access controls. Data from one client is never cross-referenced or mixed with data from another client to prevent competitive intelligence leakage and ensure forensic integrity.

We employ industry-standard security measures including:

• End-to-end encryption for data in transit (TLS 1.3)
• Encryption at rest for temporary storage (AES-256)
• Role-based access control (RBAC) for internal systems
• Regular security audits and penetration testing
• Zero-trust network architecture

4. PII Exclusion Policy

Our protocols are designed to operate without personal data. Hardware attestation relies on device-level cryptographic identities, not user identities. Telemetry verification analyzes physical motion patterns, not individual behavior tracking.

Any incidental personal data present in submitted telemetry logs (e.g., location data) is processed solely for fraud detection and integrity verification under legitimate interest exemptions and is not retained beyond the analysis period.

5. Regulatory Compliance

India - Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP) 2023: Orthonode Infrastructure Labs complies with the DPDP Act 2023. Our protocols do not intentionally process personal data as defined under Indian law. Where data processing occurs, it is under lawful exemptions for fraud prevention, security research, and technical necessity.

International Operations: For clients outside India, we adhere to applicable data protection regulations including GDPR (EU), CCPA (California), and equivalent frameworks through contractual data processing agreements.

6. Third-Party Services

We utilize the following third-party infrastructure providers:

• Arbitrum Network: Blockchain infrastructure (decentralized, no PII)
• GitHub: Code repository hosting (no customer data)
• Cloud Compute Providers: Isolated verification environments (encrypted, temporary)

These providers are bound by their respective privacy policies and data processing agreements. We do not share customer data with third parties for marketing, analytics, or non-essential purposes.

8. Data Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting customer data, we will notify affected parties within 72 hours of discovery, in compliance with applicable regulations. Notifications will include the nature of the breach, affected data categories, and remediation steps taken.

9. Cookies & Tracking

Our website (orthonode.xyz) does not use cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics scripts. We do not track visitor behavior across sites. Access logs are retained for 7 days for security monitoring purposes only (IP addresses, timestamps, requested URLs).

10. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed at individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect data from minors. If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a minor, contact us immediately for deletion.
