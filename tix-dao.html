// ve$TICK_TOKEN_MODEL : LONG_TERM_STAKEHOLDERS_WIN

The ve$TICK (vote-escrowed $TICK) model gives long-term stakeholders — fans and artists genuinely invested in a venue — disproportionately higher voting power. Flash loans require a 30-day minimum lock, making governance capture by borrowed capital structurally impossible.

Analogous to Curve Finance's veCRV but purpose-built for Solana's SPL-Governance voter weight plugin interface. Reusable by any Realms DAO — not just TIX-DAO venues.

7-day voting cooloff after quorum reached — council can veto before execution. Even a 30-day token accumulator cannot push through malicious proposals instantly.

// ARCHITECTURE : WHY_SPL_GOVERNANCE_REALMS

No Custom Contract Risk
Built on Audited Realms
SPL-Governance is Solana Labs' canonical governance program — externally audited. TIX-DAO uses zero custom on-chain programs in Phase 1. The proposal lifecycle, vote weight calculation, veto authority, and council multi-sig are all battle-tested primitives.
Flash Loan Proof by Design
30d Lock + 7d Cooloff
The $182M Beanstalk exploit succeeded because there was no lock period. TIX-DAO requires a minimum 30-day token deposit before governance rights activate — flash loans cannot satisfy a 30-day lock in a single transaction. Council veto adds a second circuit breaker.
Isolated Governance
One Realm Per Venue
Each venue deploys exactly one Realm on Solana — creating an isolated governance namespace. A compromised Realm cannot affect other venues. Single Realm pattern with Community Governance for voting and Council Governance for emergency veto.
